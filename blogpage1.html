WHAT IS A BOLTZMANN BRAIN?

“Memories warm you up from the inside. But they also tear you apart.”
â€• Haruki Murakami, Kafka on the Shore

But how does one know they’re real? One might argue ,”I have experienced them first-hand, I am the proof of them being real”. But many cosmologists and scientists might not be that much sure.

Ludwig Eduard Boltzmann was an Austrian physicist. His greatest achievements were the development of statistical mechanics, and the statistical explanation of the second law of thermodynamics.

During his time one prominent question for cosmologists was whatever the universe existed for a finite or an infinite amount of time? This question meant a lot for Boltzmann. He believed that the second law of thermodynamics, i.e.

“The entropy, measurement of disorder of a closed system, is always increasing.”

was statistical in nature. That means the law was the most probable outcome but not the absolute result.

Now if the universe existed for an infinite amount of time, most unlikely events are likely to occur.

Even something as absurd as a brain popping out of a pool of mass and vanishing in seconds.

This is the Boltzmann brain paradox, which suggests,

It might be more likely for a single brain to spontaneously form in a void (complete with a memory of having existed in our universe) rather than for the entire universe to come about in the manner cosmologists think it actually did.

Pretty absurd right? But if we ignore the practical sense of reality and rely on pure instincts, it is indeed more likely for a brain to pop up than a whole universe evolving in a system with ever increasing entropy. So are you just a brain floating in cosmos?

Maybe not. The widely accepted Big Bank theory suggests a finite time of existence for our universe.

And some modern cosmologists suggest slight modification of cosmic models can rule-out  the possibility of a Boltzmann Brain occurring, or at least reduce the probability to astronomically small numbers as, 1/10^10^5.

Still the possibility of occurrence is not denied totally.

And as Feynman has pointed out that, if one knows oneself to be a typical Boltzmann brain, one does not expect "normal" observations to continue in the future.
